Oxidation Logic
Oxidation usually increases C-O, C-N, C-X bonds or decreases C-H bonds. Mild oxidants stop at aldehydes/ketones; strong oxidants push further to acids or cleavage products.
PCC / Swern / DMP KMnO4 / Jones / O3
Reduction Logic
Reduction usually increases C-H bonds or decreases C-O, C-N, C-X bond order. NaBH4 is carbonyl-selective; LiAlH4 and catalytic hydrogenation are much stronger.
NaBH4 mild LiAlH4 strong H2/Pd

Primary alcoholRCH2OH -> RCOOHKMnO4, Jones reagentStrong oxidation; aqueous/acidic conditions push to acid.
Secondary alcoholR2CHOH -> R2C=OPCC, Jones reagent, KMnO4Clean ketone formation; no further oxidation without C-C cleavage.
AldehydeRCHO -> RCOOHTollens reagent, Fehling solution, KMnO4Very easy oxidation; useful qualitative tests for aldehydes.
AlkeneC=C -> vicinal diolOsO4, cold dilute KMnO4Syn dihydroxylation; cold KMnO4 is Baeyer's test.
AlkeneC=C -> carbonyl cleavageOzonolysisO3/Zn or O3/DMS gives aldehydes/ketones; oxidative workup can give acids.
Alkyl benzeneAr-CH3 / Ar-alkyl -> Ar-COOHHot concentrated KMnO4Needs at least one benzylic hydrogen; important for pharma impurity oxidation.
KetoneKetone -> smaller acidsStrong KMnO4Harsh oxidative cleavage; not a mild synthetic choice.
SulfideR-S-R -> sulfoxide -> sulfoneH2O2, mCPBAStoichiometry controls sulfoxide versus sulfone when optimized.
KetoneKetone -> ester / lactoneBaeyer-Villiger oxidation, mCPBA, peracidsOxygen insertion; migratory aptitude controls regioselectivity.
Mild oxidants: PCC, Swern, and DMP are preferred when a primary alcohol must stop at the aldehyde stage.
Strong oxidants: KMnO4, Jones reagent, and O3 can overoxidize or cleave carbon skeletons.
Pharma note: Benzylic oxidation and sulfide-to-sulfoxide/sulfone pathways are common impurity and metabolite alerts.

Reducing Agents by Functional Group
Carbonyls, acids, nitro, nitrile, amide, arene

AldehydeRCHO -> RCH2OHNaBH4, LiAlH4NaBH4 is mild and usually enough.
KetoneR2C=O -> R2CHOHNaBH4, LiAlH4Forms secondary alcohol; stereocenter may form as racemate.
Carboxylic acid / esterRCOOH / RCOOR -> alcoholLiAlH4Strong hydride; requires dry ether/THF and careful workup.
Acid chlorideRCOCl -> RCHORosenmund reductionH2, poisoned Pd/BaSO4; stops at aldehyde.
AlkeneC=C -> alkaneCatalytic hydrogenationH2/Pd, Pt, or Ni; syn addition on metal surface.
Nitro compoundAr/alkyl-NO2 -> amineSn/HCl, Fe/HCl, H2/PdClassic route to anilines and amines.
NitrileRCN -> RCH2NH2LiAlH4, H2/NiGives primary amine with one extra carbon retained.
AmideRCONH2 / RCONR2 -> amineLiAlH4Reduces C=O to CH2 while retaining nitrogen substituents.
CarbonylC=O -> CH2Clemmensen, Wolff-KishnerClemmensen is acidic; Wolff-Kishner is basic/high heat.
Aromatic ringArene -> 1,4-cyclohexadieneBirch reductionNa/NH3(l), alcohol; substituents control regiochemistry.
Mild reducer: NaBH4 reduces aldehydes and ketones but usually leaves esters, acids, and amides alone.
Strong reducer: LiAlH4 reduces acids, esters, amides, nitriles, aldehydes, and ketones; water-sensitive and vigorous.
Interview pick: For C=O -> CH2 choose Clemmensen if the substrate tolerates acid, Wolff-Kishner if it tolerates base and heat.

Super Important Summary

Strong Oxidising Agents
KMnO4, Jones reagent, and O3. Use when full oxidation, oxidative cleavage, or benzylic side-chain oxidation is desired.
Mild Oxidising Agents
PCC, Swern oxidation, and Dess-Martin periodinane. Best for alcohol to aldehyde/ketone conversions without overoxidation.
Strong Reducing Agents
LiAlH4 and H2/Pd. Use for difficult reductions such as esters, acids, amides, nitriles, nitro compounds, and catalytic hydrogenation.
Mild Reducing Agent
NaBH4. Selective for aldehydes and ketones, convenient in protic solvents, and usually compatible with many other functional groups.
