Seeing Molecules

Spectroscopy allows chemists to determine molecular structure by studying how molecules interact with electromagnetic radiation.

Mass Spectrometry (MS)
Molecular ion · Fragmentation · m/z

MS measures the mass-to-charge ratio (m/z) of ions. The molecular ion (M⁺•) gives the molecular weight. Fragmentation pattern gives structural information. High-resolution MS gives exact molecular formula.

Key fragmentations: α-cleavage (next to heteroatom or C=O), McLafferty rearrangement (γ-H transfer), allylic/benzylic cleavage (stable carbocation), retro Diels-Alder in cyclic systems.
Isotope patterns: Cl (M:M+2 = 3:1), Br (M:M+2 ≈ 1:1). Useful for identifying presence of halogens. Each ¹³C adds 1.1% to M+1 peak.

Nobel Prizes in Organic Chemistry

1902
Emil Fischer
Sugar and purine syntheses. Established stereospecificity of enzyme reactions ("lock and key" model).
1912
Victor Grignard & Paul Sabatier
Grignard reagents (organomagnesium halides); Sabatier for catalytic hydrogenation with finely divided metals.
1947
Sir Robert Robinson
Investigations on plant products (alkaloids). Tropinone synthesis, morphine structure, strychnine.
1950
Diels & Alder
Discovery and development of the [4+2] diene synthesis (Diels-Alder reaction).
1965
R.B. Woodward
Outstanding achievements in organic synthesis (reserpine, chlorophyll, strychnine, vitamin B12).
1975
John Cornforth & Vladimir Prelog
Stereochemistry of enzyme-catalysed reactions; stereochemistry of organic molecules and reactions.
1979
Herbert Brown & Georg Wittig
Organoboron compounds (hydroboration); phosphorus-containing reagents (Wittig reaction).
1984
Bruce Merrifield
Solid phase peptide synthesis methodology.
2001
Sharpless, Noyori, Knowles
Catalytic asymmetric synthesis: chiral epoxidation, asymmetric hydrogenation.
2005
Grubbs, Schrock & Chauvin
Development of olefin metathesis (RCM, ROMP, ADMET). Industrial and synthesis applications.
2010
Heck, Negishi & Suzuki
Palladium-catalysed cross couplings in organic synthesis.
2021
Benjamin List & David MacMillan
Development of asymmetric organocatalysis (enamine and iminium catalysis).
